United Towing & Transport LLC

Privacy Policy

Effective: April 16, 2026

1. Overview

This privacy policy describes how United Towing & Transport LLC ("United Towing," "we," "us," or "our") handles information within our internal operations and invoicing application available at app.unitedtowtruck.com (the "App").

The App is a private, internal tool used by the owners and authorized personnel of United Towing & Transport LLC to manage tow jobs, generate invoices, track financial performance, and connect to our own business bank accounts for bookkeeping purposes. The App is not offered as a product or service to the general public.

2. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to:

  • Authorized internal users of the App (company owners and authorized office personnel).
  • Customers of United Towing whose contact and job details are recorded in the App for the purpose of providing tow and transport services.
  • Financial institutions connected to the App by the company's owners for bookkeeping purposes.

3. Information we collect

Job and customer information. When we perform a tow or transport service, we record information needed to complete the job and produce an invoice, including: customer or partner business name, phone number, pickup and drop-off addresses, vehicle details (year, make, model, color, VIN, plate), service details, pricing, payment method, and internal notes.

Business financial information. When a company owner connects a business bank account or credit card to the App through Plaid Inc. ("Plaid"), the App receives account balances, account metadata (masked account number, account name and type), and transaction history for those accounts. All connected accounts are accounts owned by United Towing & Transport LLC itself, connected by the company's own owners.

Authentication and access information. The App is protected by a shared access code issued only to authorized internal users. Access tokens issued by Plaid are stored server-side in encrypted form and are never exposed to the client browser.

We do not collect Social Security numbers, full bank account numbers, routing numbers, or online banking passwords. Plaid's secure login flow handles bank authentication directly; the App never sees or stores a user's bank credentials.

4. How we use information

We use collected information solely for legitimate internal business purposes, including:

  • Dispatching, tracking, and completing tow and transport jobs.
  • Generating invoices and receipts for our customers.
  • Accepting and recording payments for services rendered.
  • Monitoring accounts receivable and following up on unpaid balances.
  • Producing internal financial dashboards (revenue, expenses, cash position, profit and loss) for company owners.
  • Preparing information for our accountant for tax filings and regulatory reporting.
  • Maintaining accurate records in compliance with federal and state record-keeping requirements.

5. Plaid integration

The App uses Plaid Inc. to connect to the company's own business bank accounts and credit card accounts. By connecting an account via Plaid, the authorized owner agrees to Plaid's terms and privacy policy in addition to this policy. Plaid's end user privacy policy is available at plaid.com/legal.

The App uses Plaid's Transactions and Balance products to retrieve account balances and transaction history for company-owned accounts. The App does not use Plaid to move money and does not have the ability to initiate transactions on connected accounts. Access is read-only.

6. How we protect information

  • All network traffic between users and the App is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Plaid access tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM before being stored in our server-side key-value store.
  • Access to production systems (hosting, source code, database) requires multi-factor authentication.
  • Only authorized company owners and personnel have access to the App.
  • Third-party dependencies are scanned for known vulnerabilities on an ongoing basis.

7. Sharing information

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information. We share information only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We use Vercel (hosting), Google (Sheets storage), and Plaid (bank connectivity) as infrastructure providers. Each operates under its own privacy and security standards.
  • Accountant and legal counsel: We share information with our accountant for the purpose of tax preparation and with legal counsel when required.
  • Legal obligations: We may disclose information when required by law, subpoena, or lawful governmental request.
  • Business customers: Invoices and receipts are shared with the customer of record for the job described.

8. Data retention

We retain job, invoice, and financial records for a minimum of seven (7) years, consistent with IRS and New York State tax record-keeping requirements. Plaid access tokens are retained only while the corresponding bank connection is active; when an account is disconnected, the access token is revoked with Plaid and deleted from our systems.

9. Your choices and rights

Authorized internal users may request at any time to:

  • Review the information stored about their accounts or connections.
  • Disconnect a linked bank account, which will revoke the associated Plaid access token and delete it from our systems.
  • Request correction of inaccurate records.

Customers of United Towing may contact us to request access to or correction of the job and invoice records we hold about them. Note that we are required by law to retain certain records for tax and regulatory purposes even after such requests.

10. Children's privacy

The App is not directed to or intended for use by children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The effective date at the top of this page reflects the date of the most recent revision. Material changes will be communicated to authorized users of the App.
